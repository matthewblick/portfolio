University of Worcester degree show photography


For the branding of the University of Worcester's 2014 Degree Show I was briefed to capture the image of a selected student to be the face of the course.

 

Working along side Rob Tovey, a senior lecturer and course leader of Creative Digtal Media at the university, he provided the inspiration and direction of the branding, plus final editorials. The theme of the brand would convey the idea of flourishing, as we, the students, have flourished over our time at the university. This would be shown in the use of floral imagery.

 
 

I captured the image of the subject in such a way that only the front of the face is exposed allowing the sides of the face and the body to fall into darkness, this was achieved using an advanced studio technique called flagging. The image would then have the renaissance floral painting applied to it to tie into the theme.

Model: Eglė Beliūnaitė
Camera: Canon EOS 60D
Focal length: 50mm
Aperture: f/5
ISO: 400
Shutter speed: 1/3s
Tripod: Yes
Light source: Flag filtered strobe

 

The final image with the foral painting manipulated into the photograph was used on invites and wayfinding amoungst other branded pieces of the event. Since the show, the monochrome version of the raw photograph has been used, along with the image of another student photographed at the same shoot, on a large banner, which now hangs at the university.
